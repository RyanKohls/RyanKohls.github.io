Data Analytics

Scrap and Rework Analysis

Using Python and Minitab, I analyzed 1 year of data from scrap and rework reports to determine the main factors causing defects. This is difficult due to the high mix, low volume production of Ernie Ball, as well as the high degree of interaction between factors (many combinations of models, wood types, etc.)

I developed a generalized linear model (GLM) to account for all possible combinations of products and determined which combinations contribute to the majority of scrap and rework. This allowed the senior manufacturing engineers to target specific areas for further process improvement projects, as well as providing easy-to-read, visual representations of the results to top management.

Automating the Milling Department Scheduling System

Historically, the milling department of Ernie Ball scheduled its production of roughly 10,000 instruments per year by hand on a weekly basis. Common issues with this system include:

  • Difficulty accounting for machine downtime
  • Impossible to make long term projections
  • Imbalanced production schedules
  • Slow schedule adjustments
  • Large amount of product knowledge required to make schedule

I formulated this as a combinatorial optimization problem and developed a decision support system (DSS) for the milling department. This automates the scheduling process and provides a friendly user-interface. The program can schedule 450 different instruments on 7 machines with 16 people in around 10 seconds.

The program is implemented in Google Drive to allow easy collaboration between the production planning, manufacturing engineering, and milling departments.

Research

Machine Learning

As part of a graduate-level class in operations research (IME 541), I developed a simulation that uses competing neural networks to determine the profitability of first degree price discrimination (setting a different price for every consumer) when multiple companies are competing with each other.

I enjoy exploring ideas from multiple fields and tying them together in new ways. This research paper incorporates topics from game theory (decision making involivng multiple rational agents), behavioral economics (behavior of consumers in response to price discrimination), and operations research (maximizing profits using first degree price discrimination is a nonlinear optimization problem).

The program was written in Python using TensorFlow, and uses a feedforward neural network classifier.
